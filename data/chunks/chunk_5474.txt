It is taken with morokmetpa (chilli chutney). Hentak is prepared by smashing hongngoo (Alocasia indica) along with a number of small sun- dried fishes (puntus species, phutunis or Eromus dandricus), which are preserved for about 3 months. Hentak of good quality is obtained after several number of smashings. It is an age-old practice and women prepare it nicely compared with men, because it involves complex procedures. Inhabitants of this region take it daily and it is considered as an appetizer and has good taste.
