Prickly, chaff flower Achyranthes aspera Linn. The young leaves are eaten as a pot-herb; also the plant is grazed by cattle and goats. The seeds are nutritious when cooked with milk and are a potential source of food; their chemical composition showed close similarity to that of bengal gram. It is reported to be pungent, astringent, pectoral and diuretic. It is used as an emmenagogue, and in piles and skin eruptions. A decoction of the plant is useful in pneumonia and renal dropsy; in large doses, however, the decoction or juice acts as an ecbolic. The juice of the plant is reported to be used in ophthalmia and dysentery. The benzene extract of stem bark showed significant abortifacient activity. A decoction of the roots is used for stomach troubles, and an aqueous extract for stones in the bladder. The flowers, ground and mixed with curd and sugar, are given as a medicine for menorrhagia. The flower-tops are stated to be employed for the treatment of rabies. Powdered seeds are soaked in butter-milk and given for biliousness; the seeds are said to be emetic.
