Bark greenish grey, yellow or reddish grey, fairly thick, smooth, exfoliating in thin, papery flakes, resinous inside; leaves imparipinnate, 30- 45 cm long; leaflets 2.5-6.3 cm x 1.2-3.0 cm, ovate or ovate-lanceolate, variable; flowers small, white, in axillary racemes or panicles; drupes 12 mm long, trigonous, splitting along 3 valves; pyrenes compressed, hard, winged. The tree is common at the foot of the western Himalayas, in Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Orissa, Andhra Pradesh and further south in the peninsula. In many places the tree forms almost pure forests, yielding an abundant supply of timber. Large forests of this tree occur in the Khadesh and Nagpur- Wardha Divisions in Maharashtra, and Khandwa- Nimar Division in Madhya Pradesh, and Adilabad in Andhra Pradesh.
