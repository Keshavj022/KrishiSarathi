Lenape, a cultivar developed using genes from S. chacoense is in the pedigree of many modern chip cultivars and is credited with contributing to major advances in breeding for chip quality in the late 20th century. Later on, it was removed from the market due to excessive levels of glycoalkaloids in its tubers, likely coming from S. chacoense. Many wild species like S. tarijense and S. raphanifolium have been found to give acceptable chip colour after cold storage. Wild species as source of self-compatibility: The diploid wild species are self-incompatible due to gametophytic self-incompatibility system.
