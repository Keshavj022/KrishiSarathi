Geographical indications Akhrot, akrut, akhor, krot. Common walnut, Persian walnut, European walnut Juglans regia Linn. The immature fruits and leaves are one of the richest sources of ascorbic acid. Leaves are astringent, tonic and anthelmintic. The leaves and bark are alternative and detergent; they are used in herpes, eczema, scrofula and syphilis. The fruit used as alternative in rheumatism. A large, deciduous, monoecious tree with tomentose shoots, found throughout the Himalayas and hills of Assam at altitudes of 3,000- 11,000 ft. Bark-grey, longitudinally fissured; leaves alternate, imparipinnate, 6-15 in.
