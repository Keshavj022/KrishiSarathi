A very variable, diffusely branched, pubescent or glabrous, prostrate herb, abundantly occurring as a weed throughout India, up to an altitude of 2,000 m in the Himalayas. It is also cultivated to some extent in West Bengal. Rootstock stout, fusiform, woody; stems creeping, often purplish, swollen at the nodes, up to 1.2 m long; leaves long-petioled, ovate or oblong-cordate, entire or sinuate, usually whitish and smooth beneath and rough green on upper surface; flowers red, pink or white, in small umbels arranged in axillary and terminal panicles; fruits ovate, oblong, pubescent, five- ribbed, viscid, glandular anthocarps.
